The Ostoyan Empire

The sun never rises on the Ostoyan Empire. Ruled by an aristocracy of vampires and their servants, the Crimson Court governs the nation with greedy claws, sharp eyes, and sharper fangs. What began as a beacon of freedom has become a land as dark and oppressive as any it sought to escape.

On the State and Origins of the Ostoyan Empire

By Dr. Illya Markov, Fellow of Imperial Geography and Historical Studies

Ostoya emerged as a secessionist project, founded by migrants fleeing the Bürach Empire's rigid imperial authority, religious enforcement by the Hearthkeepers, and restrictions on magical practice. Early Ostoyan society was decentralized, resource-rich, and sustained by trade routes, fertile plains, and shared resistance to external rule. For several generations, this independence proved viable.

Conflict with Bürach was inevitable. Though Ostoya successfully repelled repeated invasions through control of the Grey Spine passes—most notably at Fallowheart—the wars strained the young empire and exposed the limits of its unity. These pressures culminated during the second Bürach–Ostoyan War, at which point the Darkfall occurred.

The Darkfall permanently altered Ostoya's political and ecological reality. Seismic collapse beneath Nov Ostoya revealed an ancient necropolis, from which vast undead forces emerged. The sun ceased to rise over the land, agriculture faltered, and monstrous fauna proliferated. Imperial leadership responded with containment measures that sealed the necropolis—along with their own soldiers—beneath the capital.

Among those forces was Morgen Hund, a celebrated Ostoyan commander. His subsequent transformation and re-emergence as Klaus Vorgen the Betrayed marked a decisive shift in Ostoyan governance. With the aid of newly freed vampires, Hund eradicated Büracher military presence and restructured the state under vampiric rule.

The resulting regime, now known as the Crimson Court, consists of seven immortal nobles who exercise absolute authority over Ostoya's provinces. While their rule has ensured territorial sovereignty and internal stability, it has done so through extreme extraction of labor, wealth, and blood from the populace. Peasantry exists in perpetual debt, death does not guarantee release from service, and dissent is met with immediate suppression.

The ongoing secession of Raevo demonstrates that Ostoya's founding ideals persist in limited form. However, it also underscores a prevailing academic conclusion: Ostoya's liberation from Bürach resulted not in self-rule, but in the replacement of an external empire with an internal, immortal aristocracy whose interests are fundamentally divorced from those they govern.

The Provinces of Ostoya

Compiled by Dr. Illya Markov, Fellow of Imperial Geography and Historical Studies

Ostoya is divided into six provinces. Each is governed by a lesser member of the Crimson Court, operating with near-total autonomy under the authority of the Grand Duke. Provincial rulership is considered both a privilege and a test. Failure to maintain control is interpreted as weakness—an invitation for removal from the Court, and often from existence.

Estspine

The oldest province of Ostoya and birthplace of the empire itself. Estspine is a land of historical significance and present-day tension, caught between the Bürach Empire to the west, rebellious Raevo to the north, and the Crimson Court to the south. Its fortress-city of Fallowheart stands as both symbol and cornerstone of vampiric legitimacy.

Raevo

The northern province that has seceded from the Ostoyan Empire and reorganized as a magocratic republic. Led by the Council of Mages in Castalore, Raevo represents the last bastion of resistance against the Crimson Court. Life is harsh beneath eternal dusk, but the spirit of independence burns bright.

Terinima

The geographic and economic center of Ostoya, sustained by the Great Arteriyan River. Though the Darkfall crippled traditional agriculture, Terinima remains the empire's primary source of food through grim adaptation. Its people are pragmatic survivors, and Crowsbend serves as the empire's primary civilian crossroads.

Bezcodru

A province defined by the hostile Stillborn Forest. Rich in timber and ore, Bezcodru is strategically invaluable despite its dangers. The forest blocks even moonlight, and monstrous threats are constant. The settlement of Lunsk sits grimly on Glasswater Lake, ever vigilant against the darkness.

Umbroi (The Black Mire)

A sprawling marshland of peat, stagnant water, and decay. Travel is treacherous, and the environment itself deters occupation. The Umbroians survive through concealment, their settlements mobile and connected by secret channels. Even the Crimson Court's influence is limited here, where the mire conceals both dissent and its people.

Soma

The political and economic core of the Ostoyan Empire, home to Nov Ostoya, capital and seat of the Crimson Court. The city is starkly divided between the desperate Low Streets and the elite Crimson Heights. Beneath it all lies the Necropolis—partially sealed, perpetually restless, and the source of the Darkfall that changed everything.

Themes of Ostoya

  • Vampiric Tyranny: The Crimson Court rules through fear, extraction, and immortal patience.
  • Perpetual Darkness: The sun has not risen since the Darkfall, and hope grows scarce with each passing year.
  • Resistance & Rebellion: Raevo's secession proves that the dream of freedom still lives, though at great cost.
  • The Price of Power: From the living to the dead, all serve the appetites of those who rule from shadow.
