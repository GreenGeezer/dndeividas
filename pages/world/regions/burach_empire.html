The sun never rises on the Ostoyan Empire. Ruled by an aristocracy of vampires and their servants, the Crimson Court governs the nation with their greedy claws, sharp eyes, and sharper fangs. Located to the east of the Grey Spine, Ostoya was founded centuries ago by Bürach secessionists—migrants who fled the Bürach Empire’s authoritarian rule and the edicts of the Hearthkeepers. Ostoya was a new land anyone could call their own. Though in recent times, it has become a place as fractured and overwhelmed with monsters as their Bürach cousins across the western mountains. Of its six provinces, Raevo has split from the Ostoyan Empire. It currently engages in a civil war to uphold that spirit of freedom which founded Ostoya. Raevo aims to push beyond its current borders to liberate other provinces from the blood-drenched grip of the Crimson Court. Even within the provinces loyal to the Crimson Court, power is elusive. The members of the Court must remain vigilant to quell any further rebellion within their own regions of sovereignty. More importantly, they must keep an eye on each other. The machinations of the nobility run deep and seem to be as sustaining as the blood they drink. For average citizens, life in Ostoya is dark and often terrifying. Peasants work the land, which is loaned to them by deathless masters, and pay crushing tithes on any modicum of wealth they might collect. But what the nobility ask for in return for social order and protection is far costlier than any coins. All who are ruled by the Crimson Court fear the blood tax. There’s a particularly morbid Ostoyan peasant saying: “Only in death will your toils end.” But even that’s not true. After all, many of the dead in Ostoya are forced to toil further upon the bloody strings of their vampiric masters.
