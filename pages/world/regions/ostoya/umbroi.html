Umbroi (The Black Mire)

← Back to Ostoya

Officially named Umbroi, the Black Mire is a sprawling marshland of peat, stagnant water, and decaying vegetation. Travel is treacherous even for locals. The environment itself acts as a natural deterrent to occupation or reform.

Life in the Mire

Umbroians survive through concealment. Settlements are small, mobile, and connected by secret water channels. The value of currency is minimal; trade is conducted through necessity—food, tools, and favors.

The Crimson Court's Reach

The province's isolation has limited the Crimson Court's direct influence. While monsters and spirits abound, the Mire conceals dissent as effectively as it conceals its people.

Major Settlements

Riven

Riven, the largest settlement, physically migrates over time as structures sink and are rebuilt elsewhere. It serves as the closest thing to a permanent center the Mire has to offer.

Notable Individuals

Marchioness Iulia Cutov

Nominal ruler of Umbroi. Commands Ostoya's southern fleet and oversees naval security. Publicly marginal; privately engaged in covert diplomacy and long-term subversion of the Court.

Themes & Hooks

  • Marshland Isolation: The Black Mire's treacherous terrain makes it nearly impossible to occupy or control.
  • Mobile Settlements: Riven and other towns literally move as the marsh shifts, creating unique challenges.
  • Concealment & Secrets: The Mire hides dissidents, criminals, and those fleeing the Court's justice.
  • Naval Power: Despite its isolation, Umbroi controls Ostoya's southern naval forces.
  • Covert Resistance: Marchioness Cutov's hidden agenda creates opportunities for intrigue and rebellion.
  • Supernatural Threats: Spirits, monsters, and strange phenomena are common in the stagnant waters.
