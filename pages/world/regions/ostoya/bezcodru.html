Bezcodru begins and ends at the Stillborn Forest. Rich in timber and ore, the province is strategically invaluable despite its hostility. Rivers, mountains, and forest routes allow the export of resources essential to maintaining the empire's infrastructure and defenses.

The Stillborn Forest

Life in Bezcodru is defined by vigilance. The forest canopy blocks even moonlight, and monstrous threats are constant. Residents are blunt, withdrawn, and rarely leave their homes unless necessary. Safety is communal and enforced through habit rather than trust.

Major Settlements

Lunsk

Lunsk, the province's primary settlement, sits on the Glasswater Lake. Though a port town, it lacks typical commercial energy. Firelight is dimmed, patrols are silent, and every structure is oriented defensively toward the forest.

Notable Individuals

Baron Mihael, "The Wolf"

Fzeg warlord ruling Bezcodru. Enforcer of the Grand Duke's authority. Oversees roads, troop movement, and resource extraction. Permits his fzeg retinue to hunt freely.

Themes & Hooks

  • Perpetual Darkness: The Stillborn Forest blocks even moonlight, creating an environment of total darkness and constant fear.
  • Resource Extraction: Timber and ore make Bezcodru valuable, but extraction comes at great cost in lives.
  • Monstrous Threats: The forest is home to numerous dangerous creatures that hunt the unwary.
  • Fzeg Brutality: Baron Mihael's werewolf retinue terrorizes the populace, hunting for sport as much as enforcement.
  • Communal Survival: Only through cooperation can settlements survive the forest's dangers.
